# HPV in human skin RNA-seq — intermediate progress report

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Status:** ~29 % through a 12,200-sample screen of public RNA-seq data.

> **Update vs the 2026-05-05 snapshot.** The same 3,532 screened samples and 90 HPV+ calls — but sample provenance has been manually curated at the study level for every study contributing a typed HPV+ hit (16 studies). Five sample-categorisations were reclassified as a result; the headline numbers in §3 and §4 reflect the curated values. The pre-curation report is preserved as `2026-05-05_share.md` for comparison.

## 1. Aim

Survey publicly available human RNA-seq for HPV transcripts to characterise the diversity, prevalence, and tissue context of papillomavirus signal in skin and adjacent tissues, separating genuine clinical signal from in-vitro experimental material.

## 2. Approach

Samples are pulled from NCBI SRA via a query targeting human RNA-seq with skin / cutaneous / oral / anogenital tissue annotations (12,200 runs in the current cohort). Each sample is processed through:

1. **Quality control.** Adapter and quality trimming with `fastp`.
2. **Sensitive HPV screen.** `Kraken2` against a custom database covering 455 human-host HPV reference genomes (PaVE 2.0) plus the human genome as background. A sample is called **HPV-positive** if it has ≥ 10 reads classified to any HPV taxon.
3. **Type assignment.** HPV-positive samples are aligned with `STAR` against the concatenated HPV reference panel. For every reference we compute coverage breadth (fraction of the genome with ≥ 1 read) and mean depth, and assign a type when the sample passes both thresholds.
4. **Sample provenance classification.** Each SRA run is classified into one of three categories from its metadata, then **reviewed and corrected manually** at the study level by reading study abstracts:
   - **Cell-line** — named immortalised lines used in HPV / cancer / skin research (e.g. HeLa, SiHa, HaCaT, TIGK, MNT-1, A375) and generic indicators of an established line.
   - **Engineered** — primary-derived cells experimentally manipulated by transduction, transfection, RNAi, or CRISPR, or cells expressing exogenous HPV constructs.
   - **Clinical** — neither of the above; treated as fresh-tissue signal.

The three categories are mutually exclusive and reported separately throughout. Auto-classification is keyword-based on SRA titles and tissue-source fields; manual study-level curation overrides the auto call when a study abstract reveals what the title alone could not.

## 3. Cohort overview

3,532 of the 12,200 samples (29 %) have completed the screening step. The per-category HPV+ rates in **Table 1** are unbiased estimates of the underlying within-category rates regardless of how far processing has progressed (samples are classified into category from metadata alone, before any HPV calling).

Three observations:

- **HPV+ rate stratifies cleanly by category.** Cell-line and engineered samples carry HPV signal at ≈ 12 %, six times the clinical rate. After curation the two in-vitro categories sit at almost identical rates, suggesting the underlying biology (immortalised cells more permissive to HPV replication, or constructs explicitly carrying HPV genes) is similar across the two.
- **Without separating in-vitro samples, their signal would inflate the clinical rate.** The per-category split is essential for any biological reading.
- The **1.91 % clinical rate** is in line with prior surveys of cutaneous HPV in skin transcriptomes — most cutaneous infections are low-titre and asymptomatic.

## 4. Type assignment results

### 4.1 Sensitivity to typing thresholds

The default thresholds (coverage breadth ≥ 0.10, mean depth ≥ 2) were chosen for confidence. To understand how many low-titre infections might be hidden, we swept across more permissive cutoffs (**Figure 1**).

![Figure 1. Sensitivity sweep: number of samples receiving an HPV type assignment as a function of coverage breadth (y-axis) and mean depth (x-axis) thresholds. The default operating point (0.10, 2.0) yields 14 typed samples; at the most permissive cutoff (0.01, 0.25) 31 samples are typed.](figures/sweep_heatmap.png)

**Table 3.** Typing yield at four operating points.

| Coverage breadth ≥ | Mean depth ≥ | Samples typed | Note |
|-------------------:|-------------:|--------------:|------|
| 0.10 | 2.0 | 14 | pipeline default |
| 0.05 | 1.0 | 20 | |
| 0.02 | 0.5 | 25 | |
| 0.01 | 0.25 | 31 | most permissive |

Mean depth is the dominant constraint: relaxing depth from 2 → 0.5 alone (at fixed breadth = 0.10) raises typed-sample count from 14 to 25. This is expected for cutaneous β/γ HPVs, which characteristically produce few transcripts per infected cell.

The headline findings from the type distribution:

- **Alpha-type signal is almost entirely confined to in-vitro material.** Of 21 HPV16/18 hits, **19 are cell-line or engineered** (90 %). Only 2 alpha hits remain in clinical samples — both HPV16 in study ERP141290 (titles `H04E`, `H05E`, buttock skin biopsies; coverage breadth 0.30 / 0.37, mean depth 1.4 / 2.8). These borderline hits should be verified manually before drawing conclusions about endogenous HPV16 in fresh skin.
- **Beta and gamma diversity in clinical samples matches expectation for cutaneous HPV.** Seven distinct cutaneous types in seven independent clinical samples (HPV65, 134, 182, 190, 195 and two non-reference cutaneous-like genomes) — the resident skin HPV virome behaving as prior literature reports.
- **Pipeline correctly detects engineered HPV expression.** HPV80 typed at mean depth 21.4 (study SRP539392) was traced via curation to a `pLVX_E6/E7 HPV80` lentiviral construct expressed in primary fibroblasts. The signal is real and assigned correctly — it just isn't endogenous infection. Same applies to the HPV150 and HPV208 hits in study ERP149729 (DRPLA-ASO patient fibroblast lines).

## 5. Caveats

1. **The remaining 71 % of the cohort has not yet been processed.** The HPV+ rate has been stable across the run so far, so material change to the headline rate is unlikely; the more interesting open question is whether β / γ type diversity continues to grow.
2. **Two clinical alpha-type hits warrant manual review.** ERR10293422 / ERR10293427 (HPV16 in ERP141290, "H04E" / "H05E" buttock skin) survived curation as clinical because the abstract describes fresh-tissue biopsies, but the heuristic could not parse the abbreviated titles to confirm. At the depths observed (1.4–2.8) cross-mapping or carry-over from neighbouring samples on a sequencing flow cell cannot be ruled out.
3. **Threshold choice for clinical reporting is a real trade-off.** Strict thresholds risk missing genuine low-titre cutaneous infections; permissive thresholds risk false positives from index-hopping or low-level cross-mapping. A breadth-only criterion (≥ 0.10 with no depth requirement) might be a better fit for cutaneous β / γ types, which are biologically low-titre — this is worth discussion before the final analysis.
