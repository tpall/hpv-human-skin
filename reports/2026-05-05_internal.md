# HPV-skin pipeline — intermediate findings

**Run:** `results_full_v2` on HPC
**Snapshot date:** 2026-05-05
**Progress:** 3,532 / 12,200 samples (~29%) through Kraken2 screening; 90 HPV+ proceeded to STAR + typing.

## Headline

After re-classifying samples into three categories (named cell-line / engineered primary / clinical) the HPV+ rate stratifies sharply:

| category   | HPV+ | HPV− | rate  | screened/total | screen progress |
|------------|-----:|-----:|------:|---------------:|----------------:|
| cell_line  | 11   | 125  | **8.1%**  | 136 / 410     | 33.2% |
| engineered | 9    | 64   | **12.3%** | 73 / 168      | 43.5% |
| clinical   | 70   | 3,253| **2.1%**  | 3,323 / 11,622 | 28.6% |
| **total**  | 90   | 3,442| 2.55% (naive) | 3,532 / 12,200 | 28.9% |

Engineered (transduced / knockdown / CRISPR) samples — primary cells experimentally manipulated — have the **highest** HPV+ rate, even higher than named immortalised lines. Without separating them, this 12.3% in-vitro signal would inflate any "clinical" aggregate.

**Important caveat on the pooled rate.** Processing order is not uniform across categories — in-vitro material is over-represented in the screened cohort (33–44% complete) vs clinical (29% complete). The naive 2.55% pooled rate is therefore biased upward. Post-stratifying by full-cohort category proportions:

| category   | full-cohort weight | within-cat rate | contribution |
|------------|------:|------:|------:|
| cell_line  |  3.4% |  8.1% | 0.27% |
| engineered |  1.4% | 12.3% | 0.17% |
| clinical   | 95.3% |  2.1% | 2.01% |
| **post-stratified overall** | | | **2.45%** |

Projected total HPV+ at completion: **~299 samples** (~209 still to detect, mostly in clinical). Per-category rates are conditional on category-from-metadata classification, so they're unbiased estimators of the true within-category rates regardless of processing progress.

## Methods updates committed in this snapshot

Cell-line classifier (`bin/cell_line_patterns.py`) revisions, deployed via `d986793`:

1. **Added MNT-1** to `NAMED_CELL_LINES` (melanoma line; 4 samples in DRP013144 missed previously).
2. **New `is_engineered` boolean** + `ENGINEERED_PATTERNS` covering shRNA / siRNA / sgRNA / CRISPR / knockdown / lentiviral / stable transfection, plus the lowercase-prefix-uppercase-target convention (`shTFPI2`, `siSIRT7`, `LV-STK38`).
3. **Boundary fix in `_build_compiled`**: switched `\b...\b` to `(?<![A-Za-z0-9])...(?![A-Za-z0-9])` because `_` is a word character, so the old pattern failed to match cell-line names inside underscore-suffixed titles like `MNT-1_WT1`. Fix recovered 42 additional cell-line hits across 12,200 samples (368 → 410, 3.4%) and 54 additional engineered hits (130 → 184, 1.5%).

Both `parse_metadata.py` (pipeline) and `flag_cell_lines.py` (post-hoc) emit the new `is_engineered` column. The R analysis script `bin/sweep.R` reads `metadata/cell_line_flags.tsv` and stratifies by all three categories.

## Open questions

1. **Heuristic blind spots.** The `sh\d+` / `si\d+` pattern (numbered constructs targeting the same gene) is deliberately not matched — too high a false-positive rate against SH3-domain protein names. The 2 SRR32387020/21 misses confirm this is a gap; a manual study-level curation pass on the ~50 HPV+ alpha-type-yielding studies would be more reliable than further pattern tuning.
2. **Threshold choice for clinical reporting.** At the default (0.10 / 2.0) only 5 clinical samples type; at the permissive cutoff 14 do, but several have mean depth < 1 (e.g. SRR36658897 HPV195 at 0.27, SRR32454251 HPV134 at 0.25). A breadth-only threshold (≥0.10, any depth) might better capture true cutaneous infections without the depth penalty that disproportionately hits beta/gamma types.
3. **Remaining 71% of samples.** The HPV+ rate has been very stable across the run; no reason to expect it to change materially in the next ~8,700 samples. The interesting question is whether the beta/gamma type diversity continues to expand.
